How SpiceChain Works

From harvest to buyer every step is recorded and verifiable.

1

Farmer Registers

The farmer registers their farm and records each spice harvest with details like quantity and date.

2

QR Code Generated

A unique QR code is generated for each batch, printed and attached to the packaging.

3

Trader Updates

As the batch moves through processing and dispatch, traders update its status in the system.

4

Buyer Verifies

Any buyer can scan the QR code to see the full history of the spice batch no login required.

About SpiceChain Zanzibar

Built for Zanzibar farmers, traders, and buyers.

Our Mission

Zanzibar has been known as the Island of Spices for centuries. Cloves, cinnamon, black pepper, cardamom, nutmeg, and turmeric have formed the backbone of the island's agricultural economy for generations.

Despite this rich history, the spice supply chain has remained largely manual and difficult to verify. SpiceChain Zanzibar was built to change that giving every spice batch a digital identity that travels with it from the farm all the way to the final buyer.

Our platform supports smallholder farmers, processors, traders, and export buyers in Unguja and Pemba.

What We Offer

  • Digital farm and batch registration
  • QR code generation for every spice batch
  • Real time supply chain status updates
  • Public batch verification no login needed
  • Bilingual support: English and Swahili
  • Role-based access for farmers, traders, admins
  • Secure and transparent record keeping
